🌵Funny Cactus Story🌵

Once upon a time, in a hot, dry desert, lived a cactus named Captain Spike. He was round, green, and very proud of his sharp and shiny needles.

Captain Spike had two best friends: – Lola, a tall skinny cactus who loved to dance in the wind, – and Tiny, a baby cactus who was afraid of butterflies.

One day, Captain Spike found a strange thing in the sand. It was big, green, and round — just like him! He shouted, “Oh no! A twin? Am I not special anymore?!”

Lola looked closer and said, “No, silly! That’s a watermelon!”

Captain Spike got very excited. “Great! Let’s open it and have a party!”

But... there was a problem. None of them had hands. Or teeth. Or a knife. 😅

They all stared at the watermelon. The watermelon stared back (well, maybe). Tiny said, “Maybe if we roll on it, it will open!”

So they rolled. And rolled. And rolled... But the watermelon did not open. Instead, Tiny got stuck inside a tumbleweed.

“Oh no! Tiny is gone!” cried Lola.

Captain Spike had an idea. “Let’s ask the desert owl. He is very wise.”

The owl flew down, looked at the watermelon, and said: “You are cactuses. You don’t eat watermelon. You store water! You are the water!”

Everyone was quiet.

Then Captain Spike said, “That’s the saddest party ever.”

But the owl smiled. “Not if you dance!”

So that night, under the stars, the cactuses danced around the watermelon. Tiny got out of the tumbleweed, and even the owl joined the fun.

And from that day, the desert had a new rule: No hands, no problem — just dance! 💃🌵🕺
